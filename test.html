Dear Big Oil...

Please, just go away. You’ve had a good run of it! Well over a century of money, wealth, and power. More than you could possibly ever use or spend. The only cost was extracting some chemicals from the earth. Over a billion (BILLION) gallons of oil were used just by Americans, each and every day. That’s over 1000 olympic sized swimming pools, filled to the brim with crude oil, set on fire, every single day. This is lunacy, and it cannot continue. We have known for decades (And one enterprising individual was writing to the NYT about it well over a century ago) that burning fossil fuels will change the environment, and not for the better.

Big Oil has not been a good custodian of the resources they have been given. With oil pipelines, a spill is not a matter of if but when. When an oil platform off the coast of Long Beach ruptured a pipe in October 2021, more than 126,000 gallons of oil poured onto pristine California beaches, because we can’t wrench our country away from oil dependency. How many gallons of oil do big rig trucks consume, big rig trucks that could very well be using rail infrastructure? How many gallons of oil do we consume driving ourselves to and from work every day? We see the consequences of the failure to act. Those consequences look like dead birds and disgusting waters.

This isn’t to say that this drilling should just take place in some other, far-flung country, where the pollution suddenly becomes their problem. An oil spill is just as toxic and destructive when it takes place in Saudi Arabia as it is in Fargo, ND. At every stage of its life, oil is a disgusting chemical. While its being extracted, the heavy industry required to get to it and the dangers of spills make crude oil a product few people would want in their own backyard. After it has been refined and turned into gasoline, the carbon fumes are uncomfortable or dangerous to be near,
